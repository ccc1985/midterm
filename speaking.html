Speaking

分段課文內容 (中文翻譯請參考影片內容)

Paragraph 1

September 17 — feeling excited.

I have decided to run a new restaurant, and I want to make it stand out from the other restaurants. This may sound ambitious, but I am confident in myself. I have a plan that will guarantee my success.

Paragraph 2

September 19 — ranking: 1,452

I registered on Foodies* Guide, a popular restaurant review website. I made some fine dishes, such as a baked lobster* with cheese, a cherry* pie, and a special cocktail*. Then I posted some photos of these dishes with descriptions of them. I believe they will be attractive to many food reviewers.

Paragraph 3

October 9 — ranking: 817

My restaurant has become more popular little by little. Every week, I released some new dishes online. These fancy photos and names get a lot of attention. As a result, I started to get calls from people, but I told them that we were fully booked for the next three months.

Paragraph 4

November 21 — ranking: 121

My restaurant has been dominating Foodies Guide. I’ve received more than 200 reviews, and the overall score is 4.9! I also invited some special guests to my restaurant. They were satisfied with my restaurant and gave me 5 stars. I continued to get more phone calls. Some people even said that they would like to reserve a table no matter how long they might have to wait!

Paragraph 5

December 10 — ranking: 1

A miracle happened. My restaurant was finally rated number 1 on Foodies Guide! All those famous chefs must really envy me right now! Honestly, it took less time than I had expected. I thought that it would take at least six months to achieve this. No matter where people might be, they are calling to book a table at my restaurant. This is the magic of the Internet!

Paragraph 6

December 11 — feeling crazy.

I have to admit something. If you would like to visit my restaurant, I am sorry to let you know that you will never get the chance to do so. Here is the reason: I played a trick on everyone. My restaurant does NOT even exist!

Paragraph 7-9

     Everything I posted was fake. The lobster was plastic and the organic cherry was rotten. Never did anyone notice that my cocktail was actually blue detergent*. Most of the reviews were written by my friends and me. We created hundreds of accounts to post reviews. The “special guests” were actually paid, so of course they gave me 5 stars without eating anything.

     I am sorry that I misled you. However, I did this on purpose: I hope this story will remind you of the danger of the Internet. Exaggerated descriptions, made-up* posts, and fake pictures are everywhere. We are overwhelmed by excessive amounts of information every day, but rarely do we think that what we see on the Internet might not be real.

     Next time you see something interesting online, make sure to search further. Never forget that the Internet might be deceiving you, just like my fake number one restaurant!
